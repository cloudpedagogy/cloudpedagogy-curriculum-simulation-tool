# Curriculum Simulation Tool — User Instructions

---
### 2. What This Tool Does
This application models and visualizes the student experience by simulating workload distribution, assessment clustering, and skill progression over a 12-week teaching block. It helps highlight pressure points, such as weeks with overlapping deadlines or unrealistic hourly demands.

---
### 3. Role in the Ecosystem
- **Phase:** Phase 1 — Curriculum Spine
- **Role:** Modeling student workload, assessment clustering, and skill growth.
- **Reference:** [../SYSTEM_OVERVIEW.md](../SYSTEM_OVERVIEW.md)

---
### 4. When to Use This Tool
- When you want to evaluate if a designed curriculum is practically viable for students.
- To visualize how different student personas (e.g., full-time vs. part-time) experience workload pressure.
- To identify structural risks like assessment congestion before finalizing course documentation.

---
### 5. Inputs
- Requires a structured curriculum dataset (JSON format), typically generated by the Mapping Engine.

---
### 6. How to Use (Step-by-Step)
1. Load your curriculum dataset using the import controls (or start with a baseline demo).
2. Select a target student persona from the active persona indicators (e.g., Full-time, Part-time).
3. Review the Workload Heatmap for "Overload" or "Spike" warnings.
4. Check the Assessment Pattern section for overlapping deadlines or clustering risks.
5. Use the "Scenario Editor" to test modifications (e.g., moving a deadline from Week 6 to Week 8) and observe the impact on student stress.
6. Compare your modified scenario against the baseline curriculum.

---
### 7. Key Outputs
- Actionable risk signals regarding curriculum viability (workload spikes, assessment congestion).
- Interactive visual maps of weekly hours, skill growth trajectories, and baseline vs. scenario impact analysis.

---
### 8. How It Connects to Other Tools
- **Upstream:** Consumes curriculum data from the **Curriculum Alignment Mapping Engine**.
- **Downstream:** Findings generated here are analyzed in the **Programme Governance Dashboard**.

---
### 9. Limitations
- Simulations are based on defined estimates of study hours and assessment weights; they do not account for individual student learning speeds.
- It relies completely on the structural accuracy of the data imported from the Mapping Engine.

---
### 10. Tips
- Always check the baseline configuration before running modified scenarios to understand the initial "stress profile" of your programme.

---
### 11. Capability and Governance
- **What the tool does:** This tool includes a lightweight capability and governance layer designed to make AI use and professional decision-making visible.
- **How capability is developed:** By filling out the optional fields (like AI involvement and assumptions), you practice reflecting on the structured use of AI in your workflow.
- **How governance is supported:** The optional fields ensure that rationale, risks, and human review notes are explicitly recorded alongside the simulation data.
- **Optional fields:** All governance fields at the bottom of the interface are entirely optional. They support professional judgement and accountability without blocking your workflow.
